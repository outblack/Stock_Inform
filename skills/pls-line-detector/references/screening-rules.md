# Screening Rules

Use this file to decide whether a stock qualifies as a PLS candidate.

## Core Screen

Look for names that roughly meet most of these conditions:

- near a 50 percent drawdown zone from a major peak, or otherwise materially below the high
- volume has contracted relative to the speculative peak phase
- current market attention is low or fading
- some expectation is still alive through policy, earnings, product, restructuring, theme relevance, or renewed sector heat
- weekly or monthly charts show repeated reactions near one or more major zones

## PLS Line Rule

Only call something a valid PLS setup if you can identify meaningful repeated reaction zones.

If expectation is still alive, try to map at least two PLS lines:

- upper PLS line: the nearer reclaim or resistance zone
- lower PLS line: the deeper support or staged-entry zone

If you cannot defend two lines with real chart history, say the setup is incomplete.

## Staged Entry Rule

Only outline split-buy or staged-entry logic when:

- expectation is still alive
- at least two meaningful PLS lines exist
- the downside can be framed with a clear invalidation condition

Otherwise, reject the staged-entry idea.
